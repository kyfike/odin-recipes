Gnocchi Soup - an Olive Gardens copycat

Ingredients:

Soup

  • 2 Tbsp butter
  • 1 cup celery
  • 1 onion
  • 1 cup grated carrots
  • 1 Tbsp minced garlic
  • 2 Tbsp all-purpose gf flour
  • 2 cups cooked and shredded chicken
  • 8 cups water
  • 8 tsp bouillon
  • ¼ tsp ground nutmeg
  • ½ tsp thyme
  • 4 cups spinach, chopped
  • 2 cups heavy cream
  • Cornstarch and water to thicken

Gnocchi

  • 1 pound (about 2 large) russet potatoes
  • 1 Tbsp butter, melted
  • ½ tsp salt
  • Up to ½ cup all-purpose gf flour
  • ¼ tsp xanthan gum (omit if already
  • included in flour)

Instructions:

  1. Saute celery, onion, carrots, and garlic in butter.
  2. Whisk in flour, then add water, bouillon, chicken, nutmeg, and thyme.
  3. Simmer for 15 minutes.
  4. In the meantime, prepare gnocchis. Rinse potatoes and leave damp.
  5. Poke a few holes in each with a fork, then wrap in damp paper towels.
  6. Microwave potatoes 2 minutes at a time until tender. Flip over every 2 minutes.
  7. Allow to cool just until you can comfortably handle them.
  8. Peel off the skins and mash well with a fork or mixer, being sure to get out all lumps.
  9. Add butter and salt, then chill in the refrigerator until no longer warm.
  10. Remove from the refrigerator and add half the flour and xanthan gum.
  11. Mix with a spoon and then switch to kneading the dough with your hands.
  12. Add flour until dough holds together well when squeezed and feels firm but not dry.
  13. One handful at a time, roll dough into ropes on a lightly floured surface.
  14. Cut ropes into 1 inch pieces (or smaller if you desire). Alternatively, you can shape each gnocchi individually by hand.
  15. Bring the soup to boil, drop in gnocchis, and allow to cook for about 3 minutes or until they all float to the top.
  16. After gnocchis have cooked, reduce heat and stir in heavy cream and spinach and simmer for 15 more minutes.
  17. Thicken with starch and water if desired.
